Introduction to Continuous Robotic Learning

More recently, service robots have emerged as a highlight for both academic and industrial field. We start to see applications of robot in our daily life, such as public service robots in locations like airports and hotels. There is even greater potential in applications that are more personal, private, and customized, such as home assistant, elderly care, smart retail etc. To realize such great potential of service robots, one of the long-time technology bottlenecks is the lack of efficient and robust robot learning capability. That is, just the mainstream supervised learning (including Deep Learning) based on large amount of pre-annotated data is not good enough for robotic applications that requires learning continuously in preception, cognition, manipulation, and interaction with users. Indoor environment provides an ideal environment for robot learning especially self-supervised and continual learning, since robots have opportunities interacting with their surroundings more effectively.

The grand challenge aims to provide a platform to evaluate the latest progress of the field. The challenge is designed to have two perspectives: the perception/cognition and the physical tasks. Teams with different background in all related fields are encouraged. This include but not limited to the field of robotics, machine learning, and computer vision. Collaborations are also strongly encouraged. We believe this grand challenge will have significant social, academical, and industrial achievement in future research, and paves a way to large-scale deployment of convenient service robot in the next 5 to 10 years.

Call for contribution of local testing facilities

Since this competition needs testing at local testing facilities, and we welcome applications to contribute testing facilities from worldwide. Please email yimin.zhang@intel.com and xuesong.shi@intel.com.

There are two types of testing facilities, including full and simplified. Full testing facility may need up to 30 square meters (see the rule book for details). Simplified testing facility only needs space that can complete one of the testing tasks (especially some manipulation task only needs smaller space).

All the set up and equipment (robots etc.) should be funded by university lab or company who contributed the testing facility. Also each testing facility needs to recruit volunteers to help on the testing.

To recognize the contribution, the main contact person will be assigned as TPC member (Technical and program committee member) of this Grand challenge.
